Venues 

Overview


The Venues section is used to define all locations where tournament activities, events, or operations will take place.


Each venue can be linked to one or more Events.


Once venues are created, they can be used in Material Planning and Asset Tracking modules.


The Venue type is a mandatory field when creating a venue. You must create Types before adding any venues to the system.


Venue Types and Clusters


Venues can be organized into categories to support planning and logistics management:


  • Type â€“ groups venues based on their function, such as Competition Venues and Non-Competition Venues.


This helps structure operational planning and reporting according to venue purpose.


User access can also be assigned at the Type level, allowing administrators to easily manage permissions for all venues of the same category.


  • Cluster â€“ groups venues based on geographical proximity or logistics coverage.


Clusters are typically linked to a distribution center or logistics hub that serves all venues within that region.


This grouping supports resource distribution and actively used in the Change Management.


You can view and create venues directly within a selected Type or Cluster.


In the View by Type or View by Cluster interfaces, click Create in the Action Bar â€” the new venue will be automatically created with the pre-filled Type or Cluster information.


These classifications provide an additional layer of organization, allowing more efficient filtering, reporting, and coordination across multiple planning activities.


Venue Category


The Venue Category is a preset dropdown field with two available values: Permanent and Temporary.


This classification can be used to differentiate between:


  • Overlay and non-overlay Venues, or


  • Confirmed and non-confirmed (logically planned) Venues


This helps planners distinguish Venues that are physically established from those that are still in the conceptual or temporary planning stage.


Planners may also assign any other binary logic to this field as needed, depending on the specific planning or reporting requirements.


Venue Contact


The Venue Contact field may contain personal data.


For data protection and privacy compliance, all information stored in this field is encrypted within the system.
