Damage Reporting


Objective


To ensure all damages occurring during venue operations or the event period are properly reported, documented, and traceable through the Damage Report (DR) process.


This procedure applies to both venues with a Venue Logistics Manager (VLM) and venues without a VLM.


It does not apply to damages identified during transport or delivery, as those must be recorded directly in the Delivery Documents (DD) during receiving.


No damaged item should be removed or returned to the warehouse without a corresponding Damage Report and proper photographic documentation.


Procedure

When to Use the Damage Report


  • The Damage Report (DR) must be used to document any damage that occurs after delivery — during offloading, unpacking, assembly, movement, setup, operations, usage, or dismantling.
  • It must be completed for:
    • Any damage to assets currently under the custody of a Functional Area or Venue Logistics Team.
    • Any damage identified during venue inspections or hand-back processes.
    • Any damaged items being returned to the warehouse.
  • All damaged items must have a corresponding Damage Report to ensure traceability and accountability.


Responsibility for Damage Reporting


  • The individual who has custody of the item at the time the damage occurs is responsible for initiating the Damage Report.
  • At venues with a VLM, the report can be created directly in SM2ARTA by the VLM.
  • At venues without a VLM, the report must be completed in the standard Damage Report Form (Excel) and sent by email to the Material Planning Manager, who will enter the information into SM2ARTA on their behalf.
  • No damaged item should be moved, discarded, or returned to the warehouse without a completed and approved Damage Report.


Completing the Damage Report


  • The Damage Report must include:
    • Venue name and code
    • Logical Space or Functional Area
    • Nomenclature Code (SKU), Item Description, and Quantity affected
    • Date and time when the damage occurred or was discovered
    • Name and role of the person reporting the damage
    • Detailed description of the damage (type, location, and cause if known)
    • Circumstances of the incident (e.g., dropped during setup, damaged by usage, environmental cause)
    • Photos clearly showing the damage from multiple angles
    • Any immediate actions taken (repair attempt, isolation, marking, etc.)
  • The report must be signed by the reporting person. If report is created directly in SM2ARTA, no signature required.

Supporting Evidence


  • Each Damage Report must include:
    • At least three clear photos of the damaged item (overall and close-up).
    • A written explanation of how the damage occurred.
    • Identification of any personnel, equipment, or external factors involved.
  • All photographic evidence and notes must be uploaded to SM2ARTA (for VLM-managed venues) or attached to the email submission (for venues without a VLM).


Return or Hand-back of Damaged Items


  • Any hand-back to Functional Areas or return to the Warehouse involving damaged items must be accompanied by a completed Damage Report.
  • The Damage Report must be attached to the Room Data Sheet (RDS) or Truck Manifest (TM) used for the hand-back or return.
  • The item should be physically labeled as “Damaged” and separated from operational stock before shipment or collection.


SM2ARTA Confirmation


  • Upon submission, the Material Planning Manager reviews the report for completeness and accuracy.
  • Once verified:
    • The report and photos are attached to the corresponding item entry for audit traceability.
    • Damaged assets appear at Inventory with -DMG suffix at the end of nomenclature.
  • The Material Planning Manager may instruct that the damaged item be quarantined, repaired, replaced, or disposed of based on the severity and usability.
