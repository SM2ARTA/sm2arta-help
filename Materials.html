Materials

Overview


Materials represent all assets and items managed within SM2ARTA, covering not only traditional FF&E (Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment) but also IT equipment, Overlay elements, Signage, and, in some cases, Services.


They serve as abstract planning entities, similar to Logical Spaces, and define what type of item is required rather than a specific product or model.


For example, a Folding Chair in the Directory may represent any folding chair, regardless of color or design, unless a particular specification is required for an Event. This approach enables flexible and scalable planning, allowing requirements to be captured even before procurement details are finalized.


At later stages, Materials are linked to Nomenclatures, which represent exact assets with defined make, model, and supplier details. Nomenclatures are primarily used within the Asset Tracking module to identify and trace physical assets across warehouse, distribution, and venues.


This connection between Materials (planning level) and Nomenclatures (asset level) ensures planning accuracy, traceability, and avoids confusion during warehouse or on-site operations.


To be able to create a new Material, you must first have a Category, Subcategory and Unit of Measurement (UoM) already created in the system.


Materials Metadata


Although Materials are abstract planning entities, each material record includes detailed specifications such as a description, dimensions, and reference photos. These details help Functional Material Planners choose the most appropriate item for their needs and ensure a consistent understanding of requirements across all Venues and Functional Areas.


Each material entry in SM2ARTA also stores metadata used across different planning and analytical activities.


Metadata Field

Description / Purpose

Consumable / Non Consumable

Defines whether the material is reusable, returnable, or intended for one-time use.

Source

Identifies where the material is obtained from (e.g., Supplier, Retail, Rental, or Service).

UoM

Specifies the measurement unit used for quantity tracking and reporting.

Item price, USD

Indicates the unit cost of the material for budgeting and financial analysis.

Item description

Provides a detailed explanation of the material, its function, or specifications.

Electric (KVA)

Defines the power capacity requirement, applicable for electrical or IT equipment.

Quantity of ports

Specifies the number of connection ports available on the device or equipment.

Quantity of power plugs

Indicates the number of power plug connections available per item.

Item dimensions & Weight

Captures the physical size and weight of the item, primarily for use by Functional Material Planners as part of technical specifications or for floor plan layouts.

Package dimensions & Weight

Defines the size and weight of the packaged material for accurate logistics and palletization planning.

Pieces per package

States how many individual items are included in one package — used to calculate order quantities for items delivered in bulk (e.g., pens, pencils, batteries).

Pallet quantity

Defines the number of packages or items that fit on one pallet — used to calculate required pallets and forecast storage, distribution, and on-site activities..

Pallet spaces

Indicates how many pallet positions are required for an item in storage or transport — used for larger items that occupy more than one pallet (e.g., long tables).

Images

Stores visual references of the material for identification and approvals.

Documents

Contains technical sheets, manuals, or certifications linked to the material.

Comments

Used for any additional notes or event-specific details related to the material.


For convenience, all measurements (e.g., dimensions, volume, or weight) can be automatically converted between metric and US units, allowing planners from different regions to work with familiar measurement systems without manual calculations.


Categories and Subcategories


Materials are grouped hierarchically into Categories and Subcategories, following the same logic as Logical Spaces. This structure ensures consistent organization, facilitates reporting, and simplifies filtering in dashboards and exports.


  • Materials Category
    • Materials Subcategory
      • Material


For example:


  • Category: Seating
    • Subcategory: Chairs
      • Material: Padded Folding Chair, Black


  • Category: Workstations
    • Subcategory: Desks
      • Material: Office Desk, Standard (1.2m)


Maintaining clear and standardized naming conventions helps align planning, procurement, and logistics workflows.


SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)


To ensure clear identification, each Material in the Directory is assigned a SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) — a unique code of material.


SKUs can be:


  • Assigned manually – when an internal numbering standard exists;
  • Generated automatically by SM2ARTA – following the system’s predefined structure.


The automatic SKUs follows the formula:

CategoryCodeSubcategoryCode–SequenceNumberInSubcategory


Example:


  • Category: Furniture → FN
    • Subcategory: Chair → CHR
      • Material: 001 Folding Chair, Black


Resulting SKU: FNCHR-001 Folding Chair, Black


This structured approach guarantees that each material has a unique, traceable code across the entire system, simplifying reporting, procurement, and catalogue management.


Units of Measurement (UoMs)


Each Material has an associated UoM – Unit of Measurement, which defines how quantities are tracked, budgeted, and reported.


Examples include:


  • Each (EA) – for individual items such as chairs or tables;
  • Box (BX), Pack (PK), or Roll (RL) – for consumables or grouped supplies.


Standardizing UoMs ensures accuracy in reporting and logistics planning, improving consistency across venues, suppliers, and warehouses.


Sources


Each Material includes a defined Source, indicating where it comes from or how it will be obtained.


The source may be either:

  • A Supplier Name;
  • A Procurement Type, such as Retail or Rental.


Defining Sources helps categorize Materials for budgeting, sustainability, and dissolution purposes.


For example:


  • Procured items are typically purchased and may later be reused or donated;
  • Rental items are temporary and must be returned post-event.


Establishing Sources early in the process supports better forecasting, cost tracking, and sustainability reporting.


Catalogues


A Catalogue is a defined set of Materials accessible to specific users. Catalogues control user visibility — planners only see Materials relevant to their access. This structure helps streamline planning and reduces the risk of selecting or ordering incorrect Materials to the incorrect Spaces.


Catalogues can be organized at several levels depending on operational needs:


  • Per Project per Event – recommended for simple and detailed control;
  • Per Venue – suitable for location-based planning;
  • By Function Type – e.g., Front of House, Back of House, Technical Areas.


Each User can be assigned one or multiple Catalogues, limiting their actions to Materials within those Catalogues.


Catalogues contain Materials (SKUs), not Nomenclatures — allowing planners to work with standardized planning-level entries, while asset management teams handle specific physical assets later in the process.
