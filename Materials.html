Materials

Overview


Materials represent all FF&E (Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment) assets managed within SM2ARTA.

They serve as abstract planning groups, similar to Logical Spaces, and define what type of item is required rather than a specific product or model.


For example, a Folding Chair material entry may represent any folding chair, regardless of color or design, unless a particular specification is required for an event.

This approach enables flexible and scalable planning, allowing requirements to be captured even before procurement details are finalized.


At later stages, Materials are linked to Nomenclatures, which represent exact assets with defined make, model, and supplier details.

Nomenclatures are primarily used within the Asset Tracking module to identify and trace physical assets across storage, transport, and venues.

This connection between Materials (planning level) and Nomenclatures (asset level) ensures planning accuracy, traceability, and avoids confusion during warehouse or on-site operations.


Materials are organized hierarchically and can be grouped, filtered, and reported by Category, Source, or Catalogue, providing consistency across multiple events and venues.


Categories and Subcategories


Materials are grouped hierarchically into Categories and Subcategories, following the same logic as Logical Spaces.

This structure ensures consistent organization, facilitates reporting, and simplifies filtering in dashboards and exports.


For example:


Category: Seating


Subcategory: Folding Chairs


Subcategory: Banquet Chairs


Category: Workstations


Subcategory: Desks


Subcategory: Meeting Tables


Maintaining clear and standardized naming conventions helps align planning, procurement, and logistics workflows.

It also ensures that materials can be easily mapped to detailed Nomenclatures later in the process.


SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)


To ensure clear identification, each material in the directory is assigned a SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) — a unique code that combines category information and sequence number.


SKUs can be:


Assigned manually – when an internal numbering standard exists, or


Generated automatically by SM2ARTA – following the system’s predefined structure.


The automatic SKU follows the formula:

CategoryCodeSubcategoryCode–SequenceNumberInSubcategory


Example:


Category: Furniture → FN


Subcategory: Chair → CHR


First item in subcategory: 001


Resulting SKU: FNCHR-001


This structured approach guarantees that each material has a unique, traceable code across the entire directory, simplifying reporting, procurement, and catalogue management.


Units of Measurement (UoMs)


Each material has an associated UoM – Unit of Measurement, which defines how quantities are tracked, budgeted, and reported.


Examples include:


Piece (pc) – for individual items such as chairs or tables


Set – for grouped items such as a table and chairs


Meter (m) or Square Foot (sq ft) – for linear or area-based items


Box, Pack, or Roll – for consumables or grouped supplies


Standardizing UoMs ensures accuracy in reporting and logistics planning, improving consistency across venues, suppliers, and budgets.


Sources


Each material includes a defined Source, indicating where it comes from or how it will be obtained.

The source may be either:


A Supplier Name (e.g., CORT Events, Grainger), or


A Procurement Type, such as Retail, Rental, or Procured.


Defining sources helps categorize materials for budgeting, sustainability, and dissolution purposes.

For example:


Procured items are typically purchased and may later be reused or donated.


Rental items are temporary and must be returned post-event.


Establishing sources early in the process supports better forecasting, cost tracking, and sustainability reporting.


Catalogues


A Catalogue is a defined set of materials accessible to specific users.

Catalogues control user visibility — planners only see materials relevant to their assigned functional area or scope.

This structure helps streamline planning and reduces the risk of selecting or ordering incorrect materials.


Catalogues can be organized at several levels depending on operational needs:


Per Functional Area per Event – recommended for detailed control


Per Venue – suitable for location-based planning


By Function Type – e.g., Front of House, Back of House, Technical Areas


Each user can be assigned one or multiple catalogues, limiting their actions (viewing, planning, and reporting) to materials within those catalogues.


Catalogues contain Materials, not Nomenclatures — allowing planners to work with standardized planning-level entries, while asset management teams handle specific physical assets later in the process.
