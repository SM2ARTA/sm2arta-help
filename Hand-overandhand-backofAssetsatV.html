Hand-over and Hand-back of Assets at Venues


Objective


To ensure accurate and traceable hand-over and hand-back of assets between the Venue Logistics Team and the Functional Areas operating within each venue, using Room Data Sheets (RDS) as the official control document.


All asset movements within the venue are not recorded in SM2ARTA. Therefore, in the venue inventory view, all items will appear in compound.


This procedure is applicable only to venue with Venue Logistics presence. At the venues without Venue Logistics presence, delivery documents serve as primary documents to record hand-over and hand-back activities.


Procedure

Preparation for Hand-over


  • Prior to the start of the bump-in period, the Venue Logistics Manager (VLM) must generate or prepare Room Data Sheets (RDS) for each space within the venue.
  • The RDS serves as the official record of all assets allocated to that space and must include:
    • Functional Area name and code;
    • Logical Space name and code;
    • Nomenclature Codes (SKUs) and descriptions;
    • Quantities and Units of Measurement (UoM);
    • Consumable items are not listed in the RDS as they are not expected to be returned;
    • Asset condition at hand-over (OK / Used / Damaged);
    • Responsible person or Functional Area representative receiving the assets.

Hand-over Process


Venue Logistics Manager and Functional Area Representative should:


  • Conduct the hand-over physically in the allocated space or at Logistics Compound.
  • Verify that all items listed in the RDS are present and in acceptable condition.
  • Take photos of:
    • The general setup of the space after delivery (or items if hand-over in compound);
    • Any pre-existing damaged items.
  • Record any discrepancies or damages directly in the RDS or replace them with new items. If damage items are identified and can not be used, fill out Damage Report.
  • Once verified:
    • Both parties sign and date the RDS to confirm completion of hand-over;
    • If required, the third party (Venue Management or other Functional Area) validates the RGS with signature;
    • One signed copy remains with the Functional Area, and one with the VLM.


Operational Period


  • During the operational phase, the Functional Area Manager is responsible for monitoring the condition and location of all items listed in the RDS.
  • Any temporary relocation of assets between spaces must be recorded on the RDS and reported to Venue Logistics Manager.
  • If damages occur, the Functional Area Representative must immediately notify the VLM and fill out the Damage Report.


Hand-back Process


  • At the start of bump-out, the VLM and Functional Area Representative conduct a physical inspection of the space using the original RDS.
  • Verify:
    • All assets listed are returned and in the same condition as at hand-over;
    • Any missing or damaged items are recorded.
  • Take photos of:
    • Returned items and their condition;
    • Any damages or missing assets.
  • Record discrepancies in the RDS.
  • Once inspection is complete:
    • Both parties sign the RDS to confirm hand-back;
    • The VLM consolidates all RDS forms for the venue.


Documentation and Archiving


  • All signed RDS (both hand-over and hand-back) must be scanned and uploaded to the venue document repository under the respective venue folder by VLM.
  • The VLM ensures that all supporting photos, Damage Reports, and notes are attached.
  • The final consolidated RDS set serves as the official venue-level record of asset usage and condition during the event.
