Dissolution and Asset Closure


Objective


To ensure that all assets identified for dissolution — including rented, damaged, obsolete, or surplus items — are processed in a controlled and auditable manner.


All dissolution actions must be reviewed, approved, and executed in accordance with the company-wide Dissolution Policy and properly reflected in SM2ARTA to maintain accurate inventory records.


Procedure

Initiation of Dissolution


  • Any proposal to dissolve an asset from active use must be submitted to the Material Planning Manager (MPM) for review and approval by asset owner.
  • The Material Planning Manager must review each request in alignment with the company Dissolution Policy and determine the appropriate course of action: reuse, redistribution, recycling, donation, or disposal.
  • No asset may be dissolved without prior written approval from the Material Planning Manager.


Documentation Requirements


  • All dissolution actions must be supported by proper documentation in accordance with the company Dissolution Policy.
  • The responsible Asset Owner must complete such document which may include:
    • Current disposition of assets
    • Nomenclature Code (SKU), Description, Quantity, and Condition
    • Justification for dissolution (e.g., damaged, obsolete, excess stock, not reusable)
    • Recommended dissolution method (reuse, recycle, donate, dispose)
    • Approval signatures if required by company Dissolution Policy
    • Supporting documentation such as photos, Damage Reports (if applicable)
  • All approved dissolution documents must be stored in the event repository or relevant venue/warehouse folder for audit purposes.


Asset Preparation for Dissolution


  • Once dissolution is approved, the Asset Owner, Venue Logistics Manager or Warehouse Personnel must ensure the item is thoroughly prepared for the selected dissolution method.
  • Assets approved for redistribution, donation, or storage must be:
    • Picked, palletized, and packed in accordance with existing packaging and safety standards.
    • Labeled clearly with “For Dissolution” or the relevant category (e.g., Rental Return, Recycle, Donate, Scrap).
  • Assets approved for recycling or disposal must be segregated from operational inventory and kept in a designated area pending collection by the authorized vendor.
  • Photographic evidence of the prepared assets must be taken and archived with the dissolution documentation.


SM2ARTA Confirmation


  • Once physical dissolution is confirmed, a Stock Correction Transaction must be created in SM2ARTA by the Material Planning Manager to formally reflect the asset closure and adjust inventory balances.
  • The transaction must include:
    • Venue or Warehouse name
    • Nomenclature Code (SKU) and Quantity removed
    • Dissolution reason and method (Return / Recycle / Donate / Scrap / Transfer)
    • Uploaded documentation including approved forms and photos
  • The transaction status should be marked as “Completed” once the adjustment is completed and verified.
  • SM2ARTA will automatically update the overall stock count, ensuring complete alignment between physical inventory and system records.
