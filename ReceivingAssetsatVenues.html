Receiving Assets on Venues


Objective


To ensure accurate receipt, inspection, and documentation of assets arriving at the venue from either the warehouse or direct from supplier.


This procedure applies to both:


  • Venues with a Venue Logistics Manager (VLM) on-site, and;
  • Venues without a Venue Logistics Manager, where the designated Receiver performs all required steps manually and submits documentation via email to the registered distribution list, as they do not have access to SM2ARTA.


This ensures that all venue-level receipts, regardless of VLM presence, are properly documented and synchronized in SM2ARTA for event-wide visibility and audit traceability.


Procedure

Pre-Arrival Preparation


  • The VLM reviews expected deliveries in SM2ARTA under the Transactions interface to confirm:
    • Shipment details, quantities, and documentation.
  • For Venues without VLM, Receiver gets notification and mentioned information by email.


Based on that VLM or Receiver:


  • Prepare unloading zones and ensure availability of Material Handling Equipment (MHE) such as pallet jacks or forklifts.
  • Allocate staff to assist with unloading and inspection.


Vehicle Arrival


VLM or Receiver must perform following activities:


  • Confirm the vehicle arrival time against the planned delivery schedule.
  • Upon vehicle arrival, request the Delivery Documents (DD) / Truck Manifest (TM) from the driver. If not available, find corresponding transaction in SM2ARTA (or email) and download from there.
  • Verify that the vehicle is correctly sealed (if applicable) and seals match documentation.
  • Take photos of:
    • The truck upon arrival (showing license plate);
    • The security seal (if applicable).


Example of Truck Manifest



Unloading the Vehicle


VLM or Receiver must perform following activities:


  • Ensure the unloading area is clear, safe, and suitable for the cargo type.
  • Verify Material Handling Equipment (MHE) (pallet jacks, forklifts) is available.
  • Begin unloading in a controlled and safe manner:
    • Two-person verification for large or fragile items;
    • Handle materials with care.
  • Take photos of:
    • Pallets as they are unloaded;
    • Any damaged items including damaged packaging or pallets;
    • The overall unloading process.


Visual Inspection and Verification


VLM or Receiver must perform following activities:


  • Once unloading is complete:
    • Inspect each item or pallet visually;
    • Compare received quantities against the DD;
    • Verify SKU codes, quantities, and condition;
    • Segregate any damaged or unlisted items.
  • Clearly record findings in DD.
  • Take photos of:
    • Damaged or unlisted items (close-up and overview);
    • Overview of received stock in unloading zone.


Discrepancy Handling


VLM or Receiver must perform following activities:


  • If no discrepancies or damages:
    • Sign all DD copies and confirm receipt with the driver;
    • Retain a copy of DD for your records.
  • If discrepancies or damages:
    • Note them on all DD copies and in SM2ARTA;
    • If damage occurs during unloading, complete the Damage Report to record all circumstances (involved personnel, MHE used, packaging condition, damage type);
    • Inform sender for further instructions (replacement, return, or quarantine);
    • Label affected items as “Pending Decision” and isolate them in storage.


Documentation Submission


For Venues with a Venue Logistics Manager, VLM must:

  • Upload all documentation into SM2ARTA for the corresponding transaction, including:
    • Signed DDs;
    • DR (if applicable);
    • Photos (arrival, unloading, inspection, damages).


For Venues without a Venue Logistics Manager, Receiver must:

    • Scan or photograph all documents and photos;
    • Submit all documentation by email.
  • The Material Planning Manager (MPM) uploads the documents into SM2ARTA and closes the transaction on behalf of the venue.



SM2ARTA Confirmation


  • Once all documents and photos are uploaded, the Material Planning Manager must:
    • Review the completeness and accuracy of the submission;
    • Verify that quantities and nomenclatures (SKUs) match the delivery documentation;
    • Confirm receipt and mark the transaction in SM2ARTA as “Completed”.
