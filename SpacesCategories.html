Spaces Categories

Overview


The Categories of Logical Spaces define the top level of the Logical Space hierarchy in SM2ARTA.


They are used to group Logical Spaces according to their functional or operational purpose and serve as the first layer in the systemâ€™s three-level structure.


Categories help organize the planning structure and ensure clear alignment between different functional areas.


They provide a high-level overview of space requirements and make it easier to filter, report, and assign user access.


A good practice is to name categories based on Events or Sports (e.g., Tennis, Shooting, Football) to maintain a consistent naming convention across all planning teams.


Categories can also be defined on a broader level, such as Front of House Spaces, Back of House Spaces, Permanent Spaces, or Overlay Spaces, depending on how the organization prefers to group and manage requirements.


Each Category serves as a container for subcategories and spaces, enabling planners to structure data hierarchically according to their operational logic. This helps maintain clarity across complex multi-venue environments where similar functional spaces may appear under different event clusters or management zones.


SM2ARTA allows full flexibility in creating and managing these structures â€” Categories can be renamed, rearranged, or removed when no longer relevant. 


It is important to keep naming conventions consistent across all venues to ensure proper reporting alignment and to avoid duplication when merging datasets or dashboards.


When naming Categories, it is recommended to:


  • Use clear, short, and standardized titles that reflect the purpose or grouping of spaces;
  • Apply consistent terminology across all venues and functional areas;
  • Avoid special characters or unnecessary punctuation in names;
  • Periodically review the list to consolidate duplicates or remove obsolete categories.
