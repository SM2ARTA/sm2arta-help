Asset Tracking


Overview


The Asset Tracking module in SM2ARTA provides end-to-end control of materials used across the event footprint to ensure complete visibility, accuracy, and traceability of all assets moving through the event logistics supply chain — from supplier to final dissolution — using the SM2ARTA platform as the single source of truth.


It connects the Main Logistics Center (MLC), Satellite Warehouses (WHS), and Venues within one integrated environment, ensuring that every item can be tracked in real time throughout its lifecycle.


SM2ARTA complements, rather than replaces, Warehouse Management Systems (WMS). WMS handles day-to-day warehouse operations (inbound, storage, outbound, picking, packing, labeling), while SM2ARTA mirrors and consolidates those records for global tracking, reporting, and event-level control.


Operational Layers

Warehouse Layer (MLC / WHS)


WMS performs operational control of warehouse functions.


Key functions:


  • Record incoming shipments from suppliers or venue returns;
  • Assign storage zones and codes;
  • Manage dispatches to venues;
  • Link stock to event-level material plans.


Venue Layer


SM2ARTA acts as the primary system for venue-level delivery confirmation and tracking and ensures all inter-venue and return movements are controlled and auditable.


Key functions:


  • Record deliveries, handovers, inter-venue transfers and returns;
  • Track inventories and asset status;
  • Manage bump-in and bump-out operations;
  • Report damages and losses;
  • Capture end-of-life or dissolution details.


Required Controls


All asset movements - regardless of origin or destination - must:


  • Be recorded in SM2ARTA under the correct transaction type;
  • Include supporting documents and photos as evidence;
  • Be verified and approved by the responsible Material Planning Manager.


This ensures visibility of the 5 Ws of asset tracking: What was moved, Where it went, When it happened, Who handled it, and Why it moved.


System Flow


Supplier → Warehouse → Venue → Functional Area → Return / Dissolution


Each movement is represented in SM2ARTA as a transaction containing:


  • Source and destination locations;
  • Nomenclature Code;
  • Quantity and condition;
  • Movement type (Inbound, Outbound, Stock Correction);
  • Supporting documents (BOL, TM, AITF, RDS, DR);
  • Photos for verification and audit.


Common document types


Code

Document

Purpose

ASN

Advance Shipment Notification

Notification created in SM2ARTA prior to shipment arrival, detailing expected delivery information to allow warehouse preparation and receiving order creation.

OOR

Outbound Order Request

Request generated in SM2ARTA to initiate an outbound movement of assets from the warehouse to venues or other destinations, providing reference data for WMS shipping order creation.

BOL

Bill of Lading

Carrier-issued document confirming shipment and transport terms. 

TM

Truck Manifest

Lists items and quantities loaded on a vehicle.

RDS

Room Data Sheet

Space-level record for delivery and hand-back at venues.

AITF

Asset Inter-Venue Transfer Form

Documents movement of assets between venues.

DR

Damage Report

Captures details and evidence of damaged assets at venues.

NCR

Non-Conformance Report

Captures any discrepancies or damages for inbound deliveries to MLC or WHS.

DD

Delivery Documents

Collective term encompassing TM, Delivery Notes (DN), Packing Lists (PL), BOL, and other documents issued by external suppliers.


Roles and Responsibilities


Role

Responsibility

Warehouse Personnel

Collective term including Warehouse Managers, Stock Controllers, Supervisors, and Operators. Execute all receiving, storage, and dispatch operations in WMS; ensure accurate physical verification, labeling, and safe material handling at all times.

Material Planning Manager

Manage all information and data entries in SM2ARTA; ensure accuracy of entries; control all asset-related transactions across warehouses and venues; validate documentation and resolve discrepancies.

Venue Logistics Manager

Oversee all deliveries to and from venue compounds; manage receiving, hand-over, and return processes; ensure every movement is properly documented and supported by photos at the venues level.

Functional Area Representative

Act as the final user of assets within venue spaces; receive and return items using Room Data Sheets (RDS); confirm quantities and conditions at hand-over and hand-back; immediately report any discrepancies or damages to Venue Logistics.


Reporting and Integration


WMS Verification: SM2ARTA mirrors data from WMS to maintain synchronization between operational and event-level inventories and avoid any discrepancy.


Document Repository: All photos and scanned documents are stored in SM2ARTA for audit and visual traceability.


Exception Handling: System flags schedule deviations, missing quantities, or damaged goods for review.


Reports Provide Visibility of: real-time inventory by location, movements per venue, bump-in status, and asset register summaries.
