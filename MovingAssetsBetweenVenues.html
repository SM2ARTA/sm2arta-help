Moving Assets Between Venues


Objective


To ensure that all assets moved between venues are properly authorized, documented, and traceable using the Asset Inter-Venue Transfer Form (AITF).


This procedure ensures that all inter-venue transfers are carried out in a controlled manner, maintaining full visibility in SM2ARTA and ensuring accurate updates to venue inventories.


This procedure applies to venues with a Venue Logistics Manager (VLM) on site and venues without a VLM. Where no VLM is present, the designated sender at origin and receiver at destination must perform all steps and submit the full documentation packet by email to the registered distribution list, as they do not have access to SM2ARTA.


Procedure

Pre-Transfer â€“ Approval and Preparation


  • When an asset needs to be transferred from one venue to another, the requesting venue must fill out the Asset Inter-Venue Transfer Form (AITF).
  • AITF must include:
    • Origin and destination venues;
    • Expected transfer date;
    • List of assets (Nomenclature Codes, Descriptions, Quantities, UoMs);
    • Vehicle and transport details (carrier, vehicle number, seal number if applicable).
  • The AITF serves as the primary document for the transfer and must be referenced in all related correspondence and records.
  • Material Planning Manager reviews the AITF, and if approved, creates moving transaction in SM2ARTA. The transaction is assigned a unique number, which becomes the official AITF Number.
  • The approved AITF, with the assigned number, is returned to the requesting venue as formal approval to proceed.
  • No inter-venue movement may begin without written approval and a valid AITF.


Example of AITF 



Preparation at Origin Venue


The Venue Logistics Manager (VLM) or sender at the origin venue coordinates preparation of assets listed on the AITF:


  • Verify that all items are available, in good condition, and correspond to the AITF.
  • Ensure proper packing, labeling, and wrapping to prevent damage during transport.
  • Take photos of:
    • Prepared pallets or assets before loading;
    • Labels showing Nomenclature Codes and quantities;
    • Overall view of staged cargo.
  • Prepare Delivery Documents (DD): BOL to be provided by the Logistics Provider, AITF serves as Truck Manifest for this shipment.
  • Before loading:
    • Record vehicle arrival time and verify vehicle and driver details.
  • Take photos of:
    • Vehicle license plates;
    • Empty vehicle interior.
  • During loading:
    • Handle all items carefully and follow loading plan and safety requirements;
    • Record seal number on the DD.
  • Take photos of 
    • The loading process;
    • Sealed vehicle (if applicable).
  • If there is no VLM at the origin venue, the sender must email the signed AITF, other DD, and photos immediately after dispatch.


Receiving at Destination Venue


Receiving and discrepancy handling follows Receiving Assets at Venues procedure.


SM2ARTA Confirmation


  • After both origin and destination venues have signed the AITF:
    • Each venue must retain one signed copy for their records;
    • The full set of documents and photos must be submitted by email or uploaded in SM2ARTA.
  • The Material Planning Manager must then review all documents and update SM2ARTA to reflect the transfer:
    • Note any discrepancies if identifies;
    • Upload all relevant documentation into SM2ARTA if received by email;
    • Confirm transaction in SM2ARTA.
  • Once confirmed, SM2ARTA must show the updated asset inventories in both venues.
