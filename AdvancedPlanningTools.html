  1. Material Planning

Advanced Planning Tools


Once Material Requests are submitted, SM2ARTA provides several advanced planning tools designed to help Material Planning Managers review, refine, and finalize requirements in preparation for the delivery phase.


These features are managed through two main interfaces in SM2ARTA:


  • Logical Space Drawer – used for managing space-level features such as Logical Space Notes, Space Images, and Estimated Bump-In/Bump-Out dates;
  • Materials Request Drawer – used for managing item-level details such as Comments, Material Notes, and Planning History.


Comments


The Comments feature enables collaboration and feedback on specific Material Requests. Ideal for internal discussions, clarifications, and progress tracking during the review phase.


It allows users to leave short notes, questions, or clarifications directly within a request, making it easier to coordinate decisions or request updates without using external communication channels.


Key Principles:


  • Comments are visible to all users with access to the same Material Requests;
  • Unread comments are shown in the counters next to Event - Venue - Logical Space. This is available for two roles - Functional Material Planner and Material Planning Managers. For Venue Logistics Manager, all comments are shown as read;
  • Clicking on counter leads directly to comment Logical Spaces with comments;
  • Each comment includes the author’s name and date stamp;
  • Comments can be added, or deleted by the comment creator or a Material Planning Manager;
  • Users can mark comments as Unread;


Materials Request Note


Material Notes allow Material Planning Managers to add detailed clarifications, usage instructions, or specific conditions related to a Material Request.


Unlike Comments, Material Notes are intended for long-term reference. They form part of each Material Request entry, are displayed in the Material Plan, and are included in all relevant data exports.


This feature is available exclusively to Material Planning Managers.


Examples of use:


  • Indicating preferred models, colors, or placement details.
  • Recording supplier-specific or handling instructions.
  • Documenting special use cases or limitations.


Planning History


The Planning History provides a complete log of all changes made to each Material Request. It helps maintain traceability and accountability throughout the planning process.


Recorded details include:


  • User who made the change;
  • Type of action (e.g., add, quantity update);
  • Date and time of action;
  • Previous and new values (for quantity-related changes).


Link Physical Space


The Link Physical Space function allows Material Planning Managers to associate each Logical Space in SM2ARTA with a real physical location within the venue.


This connection helps during the logistics and delivery planning phase by aligning logical planning data with on-site locations.


In addition, each Logical Space can be flagged as an Overlay Space, allowing easily distinguish areas that are newly created or temporary and therefore have no Existing Materials assigned.


Benefits:


  • Improves accuracy of delivery schedules and routing;
  • Enables mapping of materials to actual rooms, compounds, or zones;
  • Supports coordination for Venue Logistics Teams on the ground.


Logical Space Note


Logical Space Notes provide a centralized location to record general notes, remarks, or constraints related to the entire Logical Space rather than individual items. They are displayed in the Material Plan, and are included in all relevant data exports.


This feature is available to Material Planning Managers and Venue Logistics Managers.


Typical uses:


  • Indicating room functions or setup constraints;
  • Recording venue-specific rules or shared utilities (e.g., power, internet);
  • Adding comments relevant to multiple Material Requests within the same space;
  • Tracking the Materials delivery status to this space.


Space Images


The Space Images feature allows to attach or view reference images of the venue or specific Logical Spaces.


These images support visual planning and verification of setup feasibility. Images can be updated as the space evolves during the event preparation phase.


Typical uses:

  • Documenting space layout and available furniture;
  • Sharing visual references with setup or installation teams;
  • Providing context for reviewing or approving Material Requests.


Estimated Bump-In and Bump-Out Dates


These fields record the expected setup (bump-in) and tear-down (bump-out) dates for each Logical Space.


They help synchronize Material Planning with event operations and logistics.


Key uses:


  • Define delivery windows for materials and equipment;
  • Coordinate with transport and warehouse schedules;
  • Support overall project timeline and milestone tracking.
