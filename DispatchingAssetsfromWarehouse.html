Dispatching Assets from Warehouse (Outbound)


Objective


To ensure all assets dispatched from the Main Logistics Center (Warehouse) are correctly processed through the Warehouse Management System (WMS) for operational control and then recorded in SM2ARTA for global asset tracking and event-level visibility.


Procedure

Pre-Dispatch – Outbound Order Request (OOR)


  • All outbound movements must originate from an approved outbound order request created in SM2ARTA.
  • The OOR must include:
    • Unique SM2ARTA Transaction Number;
    • Destination (Venue / Site);
    • Expected Delivery Date and Time;
    • Transport details (carrier, vehicle number, driver, seal number if applicable);
    • List of items with Nomenclature Codes, Descriptions, Quantities, and Units of Measurement (UoMs).
  • Based on this transaction, Warehouse Personnel (WHS) must create a Shipping Order in WMS, using the SM2ARTA OOR Number as the Order Number to ensure direct cross-reference between systems.
  • Warehouse Personnel must verify that the Shipping Order corresponds to a valid SM2ARTA outbound transaction before picking starts. If not, the dispatch must be held pending clarification by the Material Planning Manager.


Example of Outbound Order Request



Picking and Staging


Warehouse Personnel perform following activities:


  • Begin picking items as per the WMS Shipping Order.
  • Move picked items to the designated loading area.
  • Once items are staged, a second person must verify that all Nomenclature Codes, Quantities, and UoMs match the WMS shipping order records.
  • Ensure that items are packed and wrapped according to standards to prevent damage during transportation.
  • Take photos of:
    • Staged pallets and boxes with visible labels;
    • Overall view of the cargo in the loading area.
  • Any irregularities or mismatches must be reported to the Material Planning Manager before loading begins.


Documentation Preparation


Warehouse Personnel perform following activities:


  • Prepare all required dispatch documentation, including:
    • Bill of Lading (BOL);
    • Truck Manifest (TM) listing all Nomenclatures, Descriptions, Quantities, and UoMs.
  • Ensure that all documentation includes the correct Vehicle Number, Driver Name, and Seal Number (if used).
  • All documents must be reviewed and signed before the vehicle dispatches.


Vehicle Arrival


Warehouse Personnel perform following activities:


  • Confirm vehicle arrival time against the planned dispatch schedule.
  • Record the actual arrival and docking time.
  • Take photos of:
    • Vehicle license plates;
    • Empty vehicle interior to confirm no pre-existing damage.
  • Verify that the correct Material Handling Equipment (MHE) is available for loading (e.g., pallet jacks, forklifts).
  • Assign personnel responsible for supervising and performing the loading operation.


Loading the Vehicle


Warehouse Personnel perform following activities:


  • Load all items safely, following the load plan and respecting weight and stacking limits.
  • Use protective materials (straps, corner boards, and wrapping) to prevent shifting or damage in transit.
  • Apply seal (if required) and record the seal number on the BOL and TM.
  • Take photos of:
    • The loading process;
    • The fully loaded vehicle before sealing;
    • The applied seal with visible seal number.
  • Record any loading-related irregularities in the documents.


Verification and Documentation


Warehouse Personnel perform following activities:


  • Once loading is complete:
    • Verify picked, staged, and loaded quantities in WMS;
    • Upload photos and all dispatch documents (BOL, TM, Delivery Note) to the WMS Shipping Order;
    • Record the departure time in WMS.
  • Ensure all required driver signatures are obtained on the BOL and TM before departure.
  • Provide the driver with the necessary transport documentation.


Shipping Confirmation


  • Once all details are complete, the Shipping Order can be confirmed in WMS by Warehouse Personnel.
  • Upon confirmation, WMS automatically sends a confirmation email.


SM2ARTA Confirmation


Material Planning Manager should:


  • Once the dispatch operation is finalized and the WMS confirmation email is received, locate the corresponding Outbound Transaction in SM2ARTA.
  • Enter the following information:
    • WMS Reference Number.
  • Verify and adjust the following if required:
    • Date and Time of Dispatch;
    • Quantities and Nomenclature Codes (SKUs) matching the WMS entry if any discrepancies.
  • Upload supporting documentation:
    • Bill of Lading (BOL);
    • Truck Manifest (TM);
    • Photographic evidence (staging, loading, seal).
  • Update the transaction status in SM2ARTA to “In Transit".
