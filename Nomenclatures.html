Nomenclatures


Overview


Nomenclatures are the foundation of Asset Tracking in SM2ARTA.


They represent the system definition of physical assets that are procured or rented based on approved Material Requests and that move through the event logistics supply chain.


Every item that is stored, transported, delivered, used, returned, or dissolved during the event must exist as a nomenclature in SM2ARTA. 


Nomenclatures provide a single, controlled reference that connects planning, warehouse operations, venue execution, and reporting within one system.


Purpose of Nomenclatures


Nomenclatures ensure that all physical assets are:


  • Defined once and referenced consistently;
  • Visible across warehouses, distribution legs, and venues;
  • Traceable throughout their full operational lifecycle;
  • Governed from planning through final dissolution.


They act as the linking layer between Material Planning, Warehouse Management Systems (WMS), venue-level operations, and Asset Tracking reports.


What a Nomenclature Represents


A nomenclature represents a real-world item or group of identical items that:


  • Is physically procured, rented, or supplied;
  • Moves through one or more logistics locations;
  • Requires operational control and reconciliation;
  • Has a defined lifecycle during the event.


Depending on the asset type, a nomenclature may represent:


  • A single, high-value or critical asset, or
  • A bulk quantity of identical items tracked collectively.


Nomenclature Types


SM2ARTA supports two nomenclature types, reflecting sourcing and ownership responsibilities while maintaining a single logistics supply chain.


LOG Goods (Event Logistics Goods)


LOG Goods are assets that are:


  • Scoped by the Event Logistics Department;
  • Sourced (procured or rented) by Event Logistics;
  • Operationally controlled by Event Logistics throughout their lifecycle.


These assets follow the full Material Planning, Asset Tracking, and Dissolution process under Logistics governance.


Typical examples include Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment (FF&E).


Functional Goods


Functional Goods are assets that are:


  • Scoped and specified by non-logistics departments;
  • Sourced and budgeted by those departments;
  • Operationally owned by the requesting function.


However, because these items are transported, stored, and distributed through the same Event Logistics supply chain as LOG Goods, they must also be represented as nomenclatures in SM2ARTA and are subject to the same tracking rules.


Examples include functional IT equipment, broadcast equipment, medical equipment, or security-related items.


Nomenclatures in the Supply Chain


Nomenclatures are the objects that move through the following lifecycle:


Supplier → Warehouse (MLC / WHS) → Distribution → Venue → Functional Area Operations → Return / Dissolution


At each stage:


  • The nomenclature itself remains unchanged;
  • Only its location, quantity, status, and condition change;
  • All changes are recorded exclusively through SM2ARTA transactions.


Governance and Control


Nomenclatures are:


  • Created and maintained centrally;
  • Governed by the Material Planning function;
  • Derived from approved Material Requests.


Once active:


  • Nomenclatures become available for Asset Tracking transactions;
  • Quantities and condition are controlled only through transactions;
  • Manual or offline adjustments are not permitted.


This governance ensures consistency, accuracy, and auditability across the event footprint.


Core Principle


If an item does not exist as a nomenclature in SM2ARTA, it cannot be received, moved, tracked, reported, or dissolved.


This principle ensures that all physical assets moving through the Event Logistics supply chain are fully visible, controlled, and accountable.
