Materials Categories

Overview


The Materials Categories define the top level of the Materials hierarchy in SM2ARTA.


They are used to group all types of Materials and assets managed in the system — including FF&E, IT, Overlay, Signage, and even certain Services — according to their functional or operational purpose.


Categories provide a high-level overview of asset types across the project and make it easier to filter, report, assign catalogues, and manage user permissions.


A good practice is to name Categories based on the purpose or function of the items (e.g., Seating, IT Equipment, Signage, Temporary Structures, Services), ensuring a consistent and intuitive naming convention across all planning teams.


Categories can also be defined at broader levels — for example, Operational Equipment, Technology, Venue Infrastructure, or Event Overlay — depending on how the organization prefers to structure and manage materials across different events or venues.


Each Category serves as a container for Subcategories and Materials, enabling planners to organize items hierarchically based on operational logic. This helps maintain clarity and consistency across large, multi-venue projects where similar materials or services may be required by different Projects (Functional Areas).


SM2ARTA provides full flexibility in managing these structures — Categories can be renamed, rearranged, or removed when no longer relevant.


It is important to maintain consistent naming conventions across all Categories to ensure accurate reporting and to prevent duplication when consolidating data or generating dashboards.


When naming Material Categories, it is recommended to:


  • Use clear, short, and standardized titles that reflect the material type or purpose;
  • Apply consistent terminology across all events, venues, and functional areas;
  • Avoid special characters or unnecessary punctuation in names;
  • Periodically review and clean up the list to remove duplicates or obsolete categories.
