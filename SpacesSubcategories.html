Spaces Subcategories

Overview


Subcategories represent the second level in the Spaces hierarchy within SM2ARTA. They are used to organize spaces within a broader Category and provide additional granularity for filtering, reporting, and assigning responsibilities.


For example, under the Category “Football,” Subcategories might include Training Sites, Stadiums, and Fan Zones. Under a more generic category like Front of House Spaces, Subcategories could include Accreditation, Hospitality, and Ticketing.


Each Subcategory can contain one or multiple Logical Spaces, depending on the level of detail required. This structure allows planners to manage large volumes of spaces without losing traceability or logical grouping.


You can also view and create spaces directly inside a Subcategory — this is often the easier approach when dealing with smaller or clearly defined groups of spaces, as it allows for faster setup and more intuitive navigation.


Creating Subcategories helps:


  • Segment planning data for specific operational clusters
  • Assign user access and reporting filters at a finer level
  • Maintain logical alignment between venue planning and functional areas
  • Facilitate more accurate aggregation in reports and dashboards


Subcategories can be created, renamed, or deleted within their parent category. 


When naming Subcategories, it is recommended to:


  • Follow the same naming convention used in categories (clear, short, and standardized)
  • Use consistent terminology across all venues and clusters
  • Periodically review the list to consolidate duplicates or outdated subcategories
